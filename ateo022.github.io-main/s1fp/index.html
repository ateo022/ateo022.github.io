An Inquisition on Beverage

It has been said that coffee culture has been a part of the Singaporean lifestyle since the dawn of civillisation (just kidding, we only official existed 59 years ago!)

While some might gravitate towards an iced hazelnut toffee latte for their midday office coffee runs, you may find that any given ungentrified individual might prefer the humble kopi.

Well now, what exactly is kopi, and where might we find it if not at a cafe?

Nanyang Coffee, more fondly known as Kopi, is a traditional coffee beverage commonly found in maritime Southeast Asian nations. Its name is derived from the Malay language, one of the most commonly used vernacular in the historial region. The beverage is typically brewed with roasted ground coffee beans in a sock. Due to its high caffine strength. it is commonly served with sugar and/or milk-based condiments, though there are coffee lunatics fanatics who enjoy the taste of pure black coffee.
